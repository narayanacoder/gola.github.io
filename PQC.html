Why it matters

Quantum threat

Algorithms like RSA and ECC can be broken by future quantum computers, making today’s encrypted data unsafe.

Harvest now, decrypt later

Attackers can steal encrypted data today and decrypt it once quantum power becomes available, exposing long-term sensitive information.

Crypto-agility

Systems must quickly swap out vulnerable cryptography so organisations can update, replace, and stay protected without disruption.

PQC Workshop Roadmap

Six essential steps toward quantum-safe readiness

Quantum risk briefing

Provide a clear explanation of quantum computing, emerging PQC standards, and the business impact of quantum threats. Establish a shared understanding of why quantum-safe planning is required now—not later.

Awareness
Inventory

Crypto inventory

Identify where cryptography is used across the organisation, including certificates, keys, applications, interfaces, and external services. Ensure no hidden or legacy cryptographic assets are overlooked during future migration.

Risk assessment

Prioritise systems based on criticality, exposure, and cryptographic complexity. Determine which assets require urgent mitigation and which can be addressed in later phases.

Assessment
Strategy

Agility strategy

Define a long-term approach for crypto-agility, covering architecture, processes, and operational readiness. Enable the organisation to update or replace cryptography quickly as standards evolve.

Pilot implementation

Run targeted pilot migrations on selected systems to validate feasibility and uncover potential challenges. Establish clear milestones and dependencies to support broader organisation-wide rollout.

Pilot
Governance

Governance & next steps

Document decisions, ownership, and governance structures to guide ongoing PQC efforts. Provide a clear roadmap of follow-up actions to maintain momentum and ensure long-term resilience.
